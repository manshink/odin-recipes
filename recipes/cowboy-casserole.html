Cowboy Casserole

Return to home page

So, what is it?

Cowboy casserole, man, it's like a big ol' hug in a dish. You got ground beef, beans, corn, and cheese all layered together and baked to perfection. It's the kind of grub that fill you up and warm your soul. Some folks like to throw in onions, bell peppers, and tomatoes to spice things up. And let me tell ya, when that casserole comes out of the oven, all hot and bubbly with that melted cheese, it;s like a taste of heaven. It's like a taste of heaven. It;s the perfect dish for a potluck or a family get-together. So next time you're in the mood for some comfort food, give cowboy casserole a try, partner. Yeehaw!

Ingredients

  • 2 slices thick-cut bacon, chopped
  • 1 cup chopped yellow onion
  • 1 cup chopped red bell pepper
  • 1 pound ground sirloin
  • 8 ounces hot pork country sausage (such as Jimmy Dean)
  • 2 teaspoons chopped garlic
  • 1 (10.75 ounce) can condensed cream of mushroom soup (such as Campbell's®)
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • ¼ cup sour cream
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • 1 ½ cups shredded extra-sharp Cheddar cheese, divided
  • 1 (32 ounce) package frozen tater tots
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley

    • Steps

    1. Fire up the oven, set it to a toasty 375 degrees F (190 degrees C), and make sure the rack's chillin' in the center.
    2. Grab a hefty cast-iron skillet, toss it on medium heat, and drop in some bacon. Stir it up until it's crispy and golden brown, takes about 7 minutes. Scoop it out onto a paper towel-covered plate, save 2 tablespoons of that bacon gold.
    3. Now, dive into the skillet with diced onions and bell peppers. Let 'em chill in the bacon drippings until they're soft, around 4 minutes. Crank up the heat, toss in ground beef and sausage, stir it up until the beef's all crumbly and browned, about 6-8 minutes. Toss in garlic, let it sizzle for a minute. If there's too much grease, drain it. Now, mix in soup, milk, sour cream, salt, and black pepper. Stir in corn, the reserved bacon, and half a cup of cheese.
    4. Sprinkle the top with the remaining cup of cheese and line up those tater tots in sweet concentric circles. You'll have some tots left, stash those for another mission.
    5. Chuck it in the oven and let it bake until the top's golden brown and the beef mix is bubbling, takes around 35 minutes. Give it a breather for 5 minutes before you dig in. Top it off with a sprinkle of parsley and serve it up hot. You're in for a treat!
